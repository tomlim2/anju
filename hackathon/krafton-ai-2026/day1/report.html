3. Problem 1-1: Hand-Coded Approach

We decomposed multiplication as iterated shift-and-add:

P = A[0]*(B<<0) + A[1]*(B<<1) + ... + A[5]*(B<<5)

Implementation Attempt & Fundamental Dilemma

We built a 1-layer model (d=24, 12 heads) with PE-based attention routing (10/12 heads achieved one-hot patterns) and PE annihilation in V projections for clean bit extraction.

DesignBit ExtractionPosition InfoVerdict
With residualContaminated by PEAvailableMLP can't compute AND
Without residualCleanLostMLP identical for all positions

The MLP's shared weights require position info to distinguish output positions, but the residual connection contaminates bit values with PE signals. Resolution requires ≥2 layers (layer 1: gather bits, layer 2: position routing + carry). Full solution estimated at ~6 hours. P_1 = −1.

4. Ablations & Failed Attempts

What mattered most

  1. d_model ≥ 32 — most critical. Below this, learning is unstable across seeds.
  2. n_layers = 2 — minimum for multiplication. 1-layer insufficient (empirically confirmed).
  3. Seed stability — tying reduces params but introduces training variance.

What didn't work

  • Low-rank (rank=2): 9.3% — multiplication needs full-rank projections.
  • K=Q tying: ~50% — symmetric attention can't distinguish operands.
  • Aggressive VQ tying at d≤24: seed-unstable (74–99%).
  • d=16 and below: insufficient capacity (37.8%).

Key Insight

Multiplication is fundamentally harder than addition for transformers. While AdderBoard achieved 6 parameters for 10-digit addition, multiplication's quadratic bit interactions (A_i × B_j for all i+j=k) and multi-bit carry propagation demand significantly more capacity. The stability boundary (d=32) reflects this complexity gap.
